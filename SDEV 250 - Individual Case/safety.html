Web Safety

The world wide web is a great place! It has connected people across the globe, allowed for online stores that will deliver items straight to your door, it enables news and knowledge to be shared instantaneously. However, it can also be a dangerous place. There are many who use the web with ill intent, hoping to catch users unaware and cause them harm - be it by damaging their computer, gaining access to their files, or learning their personal information and using it for their own gain. Much of this can be prevented with some basic knowledge of what to look for.

Scary, right? And that's just the tip of the iceberg. But don't worry! There are many people who dedicate their time to educating the masses about how to safely use the web. Here are just a few examples:

  • Browser Leaks - This website gives some great examples of the kinds of information that can be transmitted by you browser without your knowledge or consent.
  • DuckDuckGo Blog - This is a blog by the company DuckDuckGo, a web browser that promises not to track your web browsing. They have many articles with privacy-related laws and tips for browsing the web in safety. They also offer plugins for Mozilla Firefox.
  • ProtonMail - This web page explains how the company ProtonMail encrypts its user's email messages so that they cannot be intercepted and read by anyone except for the intended recipient. That includes the company itself - Not even they have access to the contents of the messages that they encrypt.
  • Mozilla - This is the home page for Mozilla. They are best known as the company behind the web browser Firefox and the email client Thunderbird. They take privacy on the web very seriously, and offer a plethora of resources to become more knowledgeable about your privacy on the web. There are also many plugins available for their web browser designed to increase your safety while browsing.
