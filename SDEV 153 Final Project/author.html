Douglas Noel Adams was born in 1952 in Cambridge, England and died of a heart attack in 2001 in Montecito, California. He is best known as the author of The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy, however he also wrote other books such as Dirk Gently's Holistic Detective Agency and The Long Dark Tea-Time of the Soul.

In addition to his writing career, Douglas Adams also worked with Graham Chapman of Monty Python on multiple episodes and sketches, wrote multiple episodes for the popular television series Doctor Who, and was good friends with David Gilmour of Pink Floyd who allowed him to play guitar on stage with them for his 42nd birthday.
